01 — THE GAMEHow it works

A shared grid of stone. Each cell has hardness 1–10. You get a water drop with random power (1–10) — you see it before choosing. Pick a column. The drop burns through stones, losing power with each one.

Everyone hits the same grid. You see what others have weakened. Carve a channel top-to-bottom — you take the entire pool. Grid refills, new round starts.

Heavy drop on a prepared column = breakthrough. Light drop on fresh rock = wasted money. This is the decision that matters.

03 — THE MODELAdaptive multiplayer pool

1

Buy a drop

Player pays X tokens per drop. 95% feeds the prize pool, 5% rake. Demo uses per-drop model.

2

Grid scales to table size

1–4 players → 6×5 grid (~16 drops to channel). 5–8 → 6×6 (~24 drops). 9–12 → 6×8 (~37 drops). More players = deeper grid = fatter pool.

3

Channel = take the pool

First player to carve a full top-to-bottom path wins the entire prize pool. Grid refills, new cycle.

4

Two ways to win

Carve the channel — take the pool. Or outlast everyone. All opponents fold? Last player standing takes the pot. No channel needed. Pure poker logic on a stone grid.

5

Fold or fight

Leave after your first drop — your stake stays in the pool. Grid depth doesn't shrink. Read the table: is it cheaper to keep drilling or to wait for others to quit?

6

Two payment models (optional)

Per drop: poker-style, pay each move, fold anytime. Per round: tournament-style, fixed buy-in, unlimited drops. Same grid, same mechanics, different tempo.
